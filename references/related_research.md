# Related Research References

## Core Papers (Max Kagan / Politics at Work)

### 1. VRscores: A Voter Registration-Based Approach for Measuring Workforce Politics
- **Authors**: Hurst, W. R., Kagan, M., & Frake, J.
- **Status**: Minor Revision at Organization Science
- **SSRN**: https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5104795
- **Website**: https://politicsatwork.org
- **Summary**: Introduces dataset measuring employee partisanship by linking U.S. voter registrations to worker profiles. Covers 24.5 million workers and 534,000+ employers.
- **Relevance to Westwood**: Methodological foundation for partisan scoring; potential validation dataset comparing employee vs. visitor partisanship.

### 2. Political Segregation in the U.S. Workplace
- **Authors**: Kagan, M., Frake, J., & Hurst, W. R.
- **Status**: Revise & Resubmit at Nature Human Behaviour
- **Award**: 2025 Dorothy Day Award for Outstanding Labor Research (APSA)
- **SSRN**: https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4639165
- **Summary**: Large-scale analysis showing partisans are strongly segregated by workplace. Democrats' coworkers are 11.7 percentage points more Democratic on average.
- **Relevance to Westwood**: Direct comparison—does employee segregation predict visitor segregation? Geography vs. selection effects.

### 3. Organizational Civic Culture: Workplaces as Engines of Democratic Participation
- **Authors**: Hurst, W. R., Kagan, M., Lee, M., & Frake, J.
- **Status**: Revise & Resubmit at Administrative Science Quarterly
- **SSRN**: https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5357023
- **Summary**: Investigates how organizational cultures influence civic participation using employment histories and voter turnout records for 28 million Americans.
- **Relevance to Westwood**: Workplace civic culture may correlate with visitor composition; organizational-level analysis methods.

## Other Relevant Papers (Max Kagan)

### 4. Business on the Ballot: Employee Voting as Nonmarket Strategy
- **Author**: Kagan, M.
- **Status**: Working Paper
- **SSRN**: https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5404794
- **Summary**: Analyzes whether firms can leverage employee political support on non-partisan ballot questions with strategic workplace implications.

### 5. Beyond Left and Right: Consumer Reactions to Corporate Sociopolitical Stance-Taking
- **Authors**: Kagan, M. & Burbano, V.
- **Status**: Working Paper
- **Award**: AOM Annual Meeting Best Paper (2025)
- **Summary**: Survey experiment examining consumer reactions to corporate positions on issues spanning ideological divides, including Israel-Palestine.
- **Relevance to Westwood**: Consumer politics—complements foot traffic approach with survey evidence.

### 6. Corporate Political Stances and Employee Turnover
- **Authors**: White, J., Hurst, W. R., & Kagan, M.
- **Status**: Working Paper
- **Awards**: SMS Strategic Human Capital Best Paper (2025); SMS Best Paper nomination (2025)
- **SSRN**: https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5104450
- **Summary**: Studies NC's 2016 bathroom bill to evaluate how corporate sociopolitical positions affect employee retention.

### 7. Trade Wars and Election Interference
- **Authors**: Brutger, R., Chaudoin, S., & Kagan, M.
- **Journal**: The Review of International Organizations (2022)
- **Status**: Published (Open Access)
- **Link**: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11558-022-09464-2
- **Summary**: Examines how politically-targeted trade retaliation increases public concerns about foreign interference.

## Politics at Work Data Platform

**URL**: https://politicsatwork.org

**Coverage**:
- 24.5 million+ employees matched to voter registration
- 534,000+ employers (private, public, non-profit)
- 1,000+ industries (NAICS)
- Years: 2012–2024

**Key Features**:
- Interactive explorers (employer, industry, occupation, metro)
- Downloadable datasets
- Methodology documentation

**Potential Uses for Westwood**:
1. Validate visitor lean against employee lean for same POIs
2. Compare geographic patterns (are high-R-employee firms in same areas as high-R-visitor locations?)
3. Industry-level benchmarks
